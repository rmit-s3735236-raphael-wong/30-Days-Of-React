
    What is component life cycles
    - Component life cycle is the process of mounting, updating and destroying a component in a React application.

    What is the purpose of life cycles
    - The purpose of life cycle is to update the react component

    What are the three stages of a component life cycle
    - Mounting, Updating, and unmounting

    What does mounting means?
    - Mounting is rendering or putting React component into the DOM.

    What does updating means
    - Updating means the component is changed when a state or props change.

    What does unmounting means?
    - Unmounting removes component from the DOM 

    What is the most common built-in mounting life cycle method?
    - render() is the most common method in mounting

    What are the mounting life cycle methods?
    - constructor(), static getDerivedStateFromProps(), render(), and componentDidMount()

    What are the updating life cycle methods?
    - static getDerivedStateFromProps(), shouldComponentUpdate(), render(), getSnapshotBeforeUpdate(), componentDidUpdate()

    What is the unmounting life cycle method?
    - componentWillUnmount
